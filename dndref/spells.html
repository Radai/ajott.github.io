Clerics have access to all the spells from the Cleric spell list, as well as specific spells granted by their domain. Clerics need to prepare spells in order to use them in a day, and can have spells equal to their Wisdom Modifier + Cleric Level (minimum of 1) prepared each day. Druids have access to all the spells from the Druid spell list, as well as specific spells that may be granted by their Circle. Druids need to prepare spells in order to use them in a day, and can have spells equal to their Wisdom Modifier + Druid Level (minimum of 1) prepared each day. Paladins have access to all the spells from the Paladin spell list, as well as specific spells granted by their Oath. Paladins need to prepare spells in order to use them in a day, and can have spells equal to their Charisma Modifier + ½ Paladin Level (minimum of 1) prepared each day. All Warlock spell slots are of the highest possible level. The total number shown is correct, but you can choose spells from any level up to the one shown for your slots. *A Wizard can add additional spells of 1st-level or higher to their spellbook if they find such spells. The number of spells known that is shown is limited to the number of spells a Wizard would have in their spellbook without copying any. Wizards need to prepare spells in order to use them in a day, and can have spells equal to their Intelligence Modifier + Wizard Level (minimum of 1) prepared each day.
